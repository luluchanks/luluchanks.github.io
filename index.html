Hi, my name is Luwin!

& I'm a product designer
that loves
simplicity. typography. food. fitness. learning.

Nice to meet you!

  1. Drawing with a crayon since birth.
  2. Dabbling with basic HTML/CSS as a tween on Myspace and Xanga.
  3. Learning graphic design and fine art in high school.
  4. Exploring design for three semesters of architecture school.
  5. Teaching intro to Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator to Rutgers Office of Information Technology lab consultants.
  6. Developing my skills in UX/UI design and front end development through Bloc Inc’s Design Apprenticeship.

I’ve always had a love for art and later on design, but my path towards design was not a straightforward one. Growing up with a family of health professionals made me think that design was a hobby, not a career. So to follow in the footsteps of my successful relatives, I worked towards becoming a physician. My high school schedule consisted of science courses, except for two courses in art and graphic design. I applied to universities with great biology programs; however, my art teacher pulled me aside and asked if medicine was something I truly wanted to do. I began to question my aspiration and applied to one architecture school thinking it was a good balance between design and science. Those three semesters in architecture school were great, but I knew it wasn’t for me. I ended up transferring schools and switched back to the pre-medical route. I took intensive science courses and graduated from Rutgers University with a degree in biological sciences. While studying for the Medical College Admissions Test, I realized I was pursuing my family’s dreams, not my own. I felt hopeless and a failure. I did some soul searching and questioned what things truly make me happy. Soon enough, I realized that design and creativity were recurring themes in everything I did, as noted in those 6 items above. A couple months after graduating, I discovered Bloc Inc’s design apprenticeship and knew immediately user experience and user interface design was the track for me. Words can’t describe the feeling of finally doing something I truly love -- exercising my creativity by solving problems through design.

Other than designing, you could catch me eating, cooking, working out, drawing, and binge watching anime! I’m always learning & always creating~
